All businesses must re-register

Kiribati's business names and companies laws have changed. All current business names and companies must re-register to stay active.

Re-register before 31 March 2027 Start early — apply by 15 March 2027 to be sure your re-registration is processed in time.
  • ✓It is free. There is no fee to re-register.
  • ✓Do it online. Use your phone or a computer — no need to visit an office.
  • ✓We will guide you. Answer a few questions and get a step-by-step plan made for you.

What are you re-registering?

A business name › A name you trade under that is not your own name. A company › A company you set up with one or more shareholders.

Not sure which you have? A business name is a trading name. A company is incorporated with shareholders.

Common questions

Why do I have to re-register?

MTCIC has moved to a new Online Business Registry to make registering and updating your details easier. The old register has been replaced, so every existing business name and company must re-register on the new system to stay active and keep your details correct.

I have already registered with the KCCI. Do I need to register here too?

Yes. Your Kiribati Chamber of Commerce and Industry (KCCI) membership is not the same as your official registration with the Government.

I have a Business Licence. Do I still need to re-register?

Yes. The Business Licence you got from your local council lets you trade. Registration with MTCIC is your official business record. You need to have both.

Can I do this at the MTCIC office?

Re-registration is done online — from your phone, computer, or other device. There is no paper form. If you are not sure where to start, use the step-by-step plan on this page and it will walk you through it.

Does re-registering mean I am starting a new business?

No. It is the same business, with the same name and the same history. Re-registering only moves your existing record onto the new system — you are not starting again.
